# Overview

Geoscience collections include sediments, soils, rocks, minerals, mineral resources, meteorites, and fossils. Together, these specimens preserve the geological heritage of their places of origin and document the history of the Earth and the wider universe. Collections of rocks and minerals provide insights into the Earth’s formation, past climates and the evolution of environments. They also document the occurrence and use of raw materials from prehistory to the present day, highlighting their fundamental role in human societies and modern technologies. Meteorites provide unique insights into the formation and evolution of planets and other bodies in our solar system. As a whole, geoscience collections constitute an invaluable repository supporting scientific research, geoheritage conservation, natural resource management as well as education and scientific outreach.


# In Switzerland

Swiss institutions are estimated to hold over seven million fossils specimens, more than one million rock and mineral specimens, along with several thousand meteorites specimens. The SwissNatColl portal currently provides access only to fossils collections.
